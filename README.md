# Swallowing difficulties in a patient with Alzheimer’s disease

## Project overview
This educational case is part of a pharmacist clinical training simulator developed for pharmacy teaching and skills training. The case focuses on medication-related problems in an older adult with Alzheimer’s disease who developed swallowing difficulties, aspiration risk, and challenges in safe oral medication administration.

The case is intended to support pharmacy learners in clinical reasoning, interprofessional communication, and caregiver counseling.

## Clinical case summary
Mr. Lin is a 76-year-old male patient with Alzheimer’s disease who was admitted with cough, choking episodes, and aspiration pneumonia. During clinical assessment, swallowing difficulties and problems related to oral medication administration were identified.

Because of dysphagia and cognitive impairment, the patient is at increased risk of aspiration, medication administration errors, and reduced treatment effectiveness. The pharmacist is required to review the medication plan, identify drug-related problems, discuss recommendations with the physician, and counsel the caregiver on safe medication use and aspiration prevention.

## Educational purpose
This case is designed to train learners to:
- recognize medication-related problems in a patient with dysphagia,
- assess the impact of swallowing difficulty on medicine administration,
- provide safe and practical medication recommendations,
- communicate effectively with physicians and caregivers,
- and support safer long-term medication use in an older adult with dementia.

## Learning objectives
After completing this case, learners should be able to:

1. Identify dysphagia-related drug therapy problems in a patient with Alzheimer’s disease.
2. Explain how swallowing difficulty can increase aspiration risk and compromise medication safety.
3. Assess whether current oral formulations and administration methods are appropriate.
4. Recommend safer medication administration strategies based on the patient’s swallowing ability.
5. Counsel caregivers on medicine use, aspiration prevention, and practical administration techniques.
6. Recognize the role of the pharmacist in multidisciplinary management of dysphagia-related medication problems.

## Core clinical issues
- Alzheimer’s disease with impaired swallowing function
- Aspiration risk and aspiration pneumonia
- Unsafe oral medication administration
- Dosage-form suitability in dysphagia
- Caregiver education
- Pharmacist intervention and follow-up

## Suggested simulator structure

### Scene 1: Physician–pharmacist discussion
The physician asks the pharmacist to review the patient’s clinical situation, swallowing-related medication issues, and the appropriateness of the current medication plan.

### Scene 2: Caregiver counseling
The pharmacist explains how to administer medicines more safely, how to reduce choking and aspiration risk, and what precautions the caregiver should follow at home.

### Scene 3: Follow-up review
After pharmaceutical intervention, the patient shows improvement. The pharmacist reviews the outcome and reinforces caregiver education and medication-related precautions.

## Key teaching points
- Dysphagia in patients with Alzheimer’s disease can directly affect medication safety and treatment outcomes.
- Tablet swallowing difficulty should prompt reassessment of formulation suitability and administration method.
- Aspiration risk should be considered during medication review, especially in older adults with cognitive impairment.
- Medicine administration instructions must be individualized according to swallowing function.
- Caregiver counseling is a critical component of safe medication use in dementia care.
- Pharmacist intervention can improve safety, adherence, and overall care quality.
