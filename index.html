📋 Case Background:
K. C. Lin is a 76-year-old man with Alzheimer’s disease, chronic oropharyngeal dysphagia, hypothyroidism, hypertension, and depression. He was brought to the emergency department after fever, productive cough, dyspnea, and repeated choking episodes during meals and medication administration. The team suspects micro-aspiration pneumonia.

The pharmacist is asked to identify drug-related problems, evaluate unsafe medication administration practices, and counsel the caregiver on preventing recurrent aspiration pneumonia. Later, the case focuses on discharge counseling and extended knowledge questions on dysphagia, aspiration prevention, and medication optimization.

📋 Case Note▾

Present Illness

Mr. Lin developed repeated coughing during meals and choking while taking medications mixed with food. He then developed fever, thicker phlegm, and dyspnea. His daughter also noted increased apathy, fatigue, agitation, aggressiveness, and visual hallucinations.

Past History

Alzheimer’s disease for 8 years, major depressive disorder after wife’s death, hypothyroidism, hypertension, GERD, and chronic oropharyngeal dysphagia. Previous choking episodes but no prior pneumonia admission.

📝 Social / Caregiver Notes▾

Lives with daughter, who is the primary caregiver. Family reports caregiver burden related to behavioral disturbance, nighttime restlessness, and feeding assistance.

At home, tablets were routinely crushed. Sertraline was crushed and mixed with yogurt. Levothyroxine was administered with meals rather than fasting. The patient often lies down soon after eating.

Scene 2: Discharge and follow-up counseling
Recurrence prevention and community pharmacy counseling
After antibiotic treatment, Mr. Lin’s fever subsided, breathing stabilized, and alertness gradually returned toward baseline. The focus then shifted from treating infection to preventing recurrence. The clinical pharmacist counseled his daughters on safe medication administration, dosage-form preparation, adequate water intake, and maintaining an upright position during and after meals. Mr. Lin was discharged on Jan 24th.
